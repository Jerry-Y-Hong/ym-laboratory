# Board Escalation Framework

## Purpose
The **Board Escalation Framework (BEF)** defines how critical alerts generated by the Board Early Warning System (BEWS) are escalated to the appropriate stakeholders, ensuring timely response while preserving the independent oversight role of AEBMS.

## Escalation Levels
| Level | Trigger Conditions | Primary Recipient | Secondary Recipients | Response SLA |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|--------------|
| **L1 – Notification** | Any **Info** or **Warning** alert (e.g., health degradation to *Warning* status) | Board Member (assigned to domain) | Project Owner (CC) | 48 h |
| **L2 – Review** | **High** severity alerts (e.g., domain risk spike, policy violation) | Board Chair & Domain Lead | Project Owner, Compliance Officer | 24 h |
| **L3 – Critical Escalation** | **Critical** alerts (e.g., security incident, risk level = *Critical*) | Executive Board Committee | Project Owner, Security Lead, Legal Counsel | 4 h |
| **L4 – Emergency Suspension** | Multiple concurrent **Critical** alerts or a **Health** score < 60 | Full Board + Executive Steering Committee | All Stakeholders | Immediate (as‑soon‑as‑possible) |

## Escalation Workflow
1. **Alert Generation** – BEWS publishes an alert to the **Alert Bus**.
2. **Routing Service** – A lightweight router examines `severity` and `category` to determine the escalation level.
3. **Notification Dispatch** – Alerts are sent via:
   - Email (templated markdown)
   - Secure internal messaging (e.g., Slack webhook)
   - Optional SMS for L3/L4 critical alerts.
4. **Acknowledgement** – Recipients must acknowledge receipt; the acknowledgement is logged in the **Board Audit Log**.
5. **Action Tracking** – The **Board Dashboard** displays open escalations with status fields (Acknowledged, In‑Progress, Resolved).
6. **Resolution Documentation** – Once resolved, the responsible party records a summary and attaches any remedial artifacts. This entry becomes immutable and is referenced in the next board meeting.

## Governance Controls
- **Escalation Policy** – Defined in `BOARD_POLICIES.md` with versioning; any change requires Board approval.
- **Auditability** – All routing decisions and acknowledgements are signed and stored for 7 years.
- **Review Cadence** – The board meets weekly to review all L1‑L3 alerts; L4 emergencies trigger ad‑hoc meetings.
- **Escalation Overrides** – In extreme situations, the Project Owner may manually trigger a higher‑level escalation via the **Board Dashboard**.

---
*The Board Escalation Framework ensures that oversight alerts are acted upon promptly while maintaining the read‑only nature of AEBMS.*
