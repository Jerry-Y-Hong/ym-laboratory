## Purpose

Define the Enterprise Explainability framework that ensures every AI-generated insight, recommendation, and decision is accompanied by a transparent, auditable, and human-understandable explanation.

## Components

### Explainability Engine Core
- Generates explanations for every AEIP output: insights, recommendations, predictions, simulations
- Methods used: SHAP values, LIME, Integrated Gradients, Counterfactual generation
- Explanations generated synchronously with the AI output (no separate request needed)
- Stored in Knowledge Graph linked to originating entity ID
- Retained for 5 years for audit and compliance

### Recommendation Trace
- For every recommendation from Decision Engine (04):
  - Step-by-step reasoning chain: data → insight → recommendation
  - Which data points most influenced the recommendation (SHAP-ranked features)
  - Alternative recommendations considered and why they were ranked lower
  - Confidence in recommendation: High (>80%) / Medium (50–80%) / Low (<50%)
- Format: structured JSON + human-readable narrative
- Trace ID linked to recommendation ID for cross-reference

### Decision Trace
- Captures the full decision journey from observation to approved action:
  - Timestamp chain: anomaly detected → insight generated → recommendation made → decision approved
  - Actor attribution: which AI model, which agent, which human approved
  - Parameter values at time of decision
  - Predicted vs. actual outcome (updated post-execution)
- Immutable audit log: append-only, cryptographically signed
- Decision traces queryable for regulatory compliance review

### Confidence Score
- Every AEIP output carries a structured confidence score:
  - **Data Quality Score** (0–1): freshness and completeness of input data
  - **Model Confidence Score** (0–1): model uncertainty estimate (Bayesian / ensemble variance)
  - **Context Relevance Score** (0–1): how closely the current context matches training distribution
  - **Composite Confidence Score** = weighted combination of above three
- Thresholds: High ≥0.80, Medium 0.50–0.79, Low <0.50
- Low-confidence outputs flagged for human review before actioning

### Evidence Chain
- Structured chain of evidence supporting each AI conclusion:
  - Primary evidence: KPIs, metrics, events directly observed
  - Supporting evidence: patterns, historical precedents, similar cases
  - Contradicting evidence: data points that argue against the conclusion
  - Net evidence weight: strength of case for vs. against
- Evidence chain stored as a directed acyclic graph (DAG) in Knowledge Graph
- Each evidence item linked to its source entity and Phase provenance

## Workflow

1. AI output generated by Intelligence Engine (03) or Decision Engine (04)
2. Explainability Engine Core triggered automatically
3. SHAP/LIME values computed for the generating model
4. Recommendation Trace assembled
5. Confidence Score calculated
6. Evidence Chain constructed from Knowledge Graph
7. Complete ExplanationReport attached to AI output
8. Published alongside original output to consumers

---

## Interfaces

- `GET /explain/{output_id}` – Full explanation for any AEIP output
- `GET /explain/{output_id}/trace` – Recommendation or decision trace
- `GET /explain/{output_id}/confidence` – Confidence score breakdown
- `GET /explain/{output_id}/evidence` – Evidence chain
- `GET /audit/decisions` – Regulatory audit log of decisions
